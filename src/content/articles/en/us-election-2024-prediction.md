The 2024 U.S. Election was one of the most-watched events on prediction markets. Instead of trusting polls alone, why not see what the market says when real money is on the line?

## Prediction Markets vs. Polls: What's the Difference?

Polls ask questions. Prediction markets ask people to bet. The first measures stated intention; the second reveals true belief — and when money is at stake, the answer changes.

On Polymarket, a candidate's price directly reflects the market's assigned win probability. In 2024, these markets proved more accurate than traditional polling.

## How to Read Political Prediction-Market Data

1. **Price = Probability**: $0.65 means the market assigns a 65% chance
2. **Liquidity matters**: Higher trading volume = more reliable price
3. **Watch trend at key moments**: Scandals, debates, and economic data releases trigger sharp probability swings

## Quantifying Political Risk

Prediction markets turn the fuzzy question "who wins?" into a tradeable asset. The core advantage: **incentive compatibility** — liars lose money, so participants have reason to be honest.
