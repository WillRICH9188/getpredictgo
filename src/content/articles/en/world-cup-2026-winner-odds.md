## Why are Spain and France out in front?

The two teams sit level because the market is pricing in their **squad depth, youth, and recent tournament form**. Spain is riding a wave of academy talent, while France has maintained elite player depth for years. Worth noting: France's probability recently dipped from around 17% to 16% after a friendly loss raised questions about its defense — which is exactly the point of a prediction market. **It adjusts to new information instantly, rather than being set before the season and left alone.**

## Why are holders Argentina only sixth?

Plenty of people are surprised: the reigning champions, with Messi, are only sixth at ~8%? The market's reasoning is squad depth and Messi's age. Argentina's price has slid from around 11% to 8% over the past month. But 8% doesn't mean no chance — it's still first-tier, and the market sees a **realistic upset threat**.

## Are prediction-market probabilities the same as bookmaker odds?

Very close, but they come from different places. Bookmaker odds are set by the house; prediction-market probabilities are **traded in real time between buyers and sellers with real money** — more like a stock price. Putting them side by side is revealing: major sportsbooks also rank Spain and France as the top two, with England, Brazil, and Portugal behind. When two independent systems agree this closely, it usually means **the market consensus is strong**. When they diverge, that's the signal worth digging into.

## What does this data mean for you?

- **Want to know who's most favored, fast?** Read the probability ranking — it beats sorting through a dozen experts who all disagree.
- **Looking for a dark horse?** Watch mid-table teams whose probability is *rising*; shifts in market sentiment often lead the news.
- **Don't treat probability as certainty.** A 17% favorite also means an 83% chance of *not* winning. Understanding probability and risk is the right way to read a prediction market.
